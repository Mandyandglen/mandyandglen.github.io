Population and Birth Rates

Global population and birth rate are imbalanced. Most of Africa is growing above 3% except for the Southern and Northern regions. North America and Europe is growing below 1.5%. The average global birth rate is 18.5 births per 1,000 total population in 2016. The death rate is 7.8 per 1,000 per year. The RNI is thus 1.06 percent. In 2012 the average global birth rate was 19.611 according to the World Bank and 19.15 births per 1,000 total population according to the CIA, compared to 20.09 per 1,000 total population in 2007.
